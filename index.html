About

I am a cybersecurity and cryptography professional with expertise spanning security architecture, standardisation, regulatory compliance, and emerging security technologies. My work bridges technical, regulatory, and policy-driven perspectives, with a particular focus on cryptography, security protocols, trust infrastructures, and secure digital ecosystems. In my current role as Coordinating Specialist Advisor at the Dutch Authority for Digital Infrastructure (RDI), I contribute to ensuring that technologies entering the European market meet cybersecurity, safety, and interoperability requirements. I actively participate in European and international standardisation activities, contributing to the development of cybersecurity standards and supporting the evolution of secure digital infrastructures and trust services. Previously, as Lead Security Architect at Axelera AI, I was responsible for defining and implementing the security architecture of complex AI systems. This included security risk assessments, threat modelling, security requirements engineering, and the development of end-to-end security concepts across hardware and software domains. I established the security architecture from the ground up and guided its implementation throughout the product lifecycle, from concept and design to successful silicon tape-out. Prior to this, at Intrinsic ID (acquired by Synopsys), I worked on cryptographic security solutions and root-of-trust technologies, focusing on secure authentication, key management, and trust establishment mechanisms for secure digital systems. Earlier in my career, I led R&D projects at imec Nederland in cybersecurity, secure communications, and digital trust technologies. My work included security architectures for critical applications, secure wireless systems, and the evaluation of cryptographic and hardware-assisted security mechanisms. Across these roles, I have gained extensive experience in the full lifecycle of secure ICT products and services, from concept and architecture through implementation, certification, compliance, deployment, and end-of-life management. This experience has provided me with a strong understanding of how security standards, regulatory frameworks, risk management practices, and technical implementations intersect in practice. I have over 20 years of experience working in international and multidisciplinary environments, collaborating with regulators, industry, academia, standardisation bodies, and certification stakeholders. I have led and mentored engineering teams and supervised PhD and Master's students from diverse backgrounds. I hold a PhD in Cryptographic Hardware Design from the University of Patras, Greece, where my research focused on energy-efficient cryptographic implementations. My professional interests include cryptography, secure communications, trust infrastructures, cybersecurity governance, security architecture, risk management, and the development of secure and resilient digital systems.
